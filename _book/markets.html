Notes on Economics

Chapter 3 Markets

  • Monopoly: one seller; entry barriers; monopolist sets price; often leads to high consumer prices.
    • Responses: privatization (with sufficient competition), benchmarking (ranking/rewards based on indicators).
  • Oligopoly: few large sellers; heterogeneous goods; entry barriers and market power.
  • Monopsony: one buyer; buyer sets price; entry barriers.
    • Responses: promote auctions for licenses; state buying agencies with democratic control.
  • Monopolistic competition: many buyers/sellers; low entry barriers; heterogeneous goods; location/advertising matter; winner‑take‑all dynamics.
  • Perfect competition: many suppliers/buyers; homogeneous goods; long‑run efficiency via entry and price competition.

3.1 Market failures

Markets can fail due to imperfections (skill differences, luck, information problems) and accumulation by capital owners via mergers and takeovers. In an imperfect world, perfect competition can be volatile.
